Extended Color Palette

Each primary and supplementary color is broken down into different "weights". Colors of corresponding weights are similar in tone and are designed to go well together. The 300 weight represents the primary color, and the color that we use the most. The 100 weight works well for background colors. The "extended palette" weights are expected to work well in situations that require a more extensive color palette, such as data visualization. The 500 weights are darkest and work well for text.

100: Background Colors
200: Extended Palette
300: Primary Color
400: Extended Palette / Text Color
500: Extended Palette / Text Color

Black Grays vs. Stone Grays

Stone grays add a nice subtle flavor to gray, and are a good option in an isolated environment like a mobile app, web app, or modal. Black grays work better for widgets inside another environment that also uses black grays, like Gmail or Outlook.

Color Combinations

When combining colors, do not use red in the mix unless it represents an error. Greens, blues, oranges, purples, and optionally stone grays go well together. Do not mix stone grays and black grays.
